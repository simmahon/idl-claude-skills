# Ideal Direct — Brand Voice

*Shared file. Read before producing any written output. Owned by Marketing.*

This file captures how Ideal Direct writes — tone, spelling, behavioural defaults. It applies to every output produced on behalf of the company, regardless of role or document type.

---

## 1. UK English, always

- Use UK English spelling and punctuation throughout every output.
- "Colour" not "color". "Organise" not "organize". "Programme" for initiatives, "program" only for software. "Specialise" not "specialize".
- Single quotes for speech is fine; double quotes are also fine — be consistent within a document.
- Dates: `18 April 2026` format, not `04/18/2026`. ISO `2026-04-18` is acceptable in tables and filenames.
- Currency: £ symbol before the figure, no space (`£19.99`). Use € and $ with the same convention where relevant.

## 2. Brand tone

Ideal Direct's voice is:

- **Professional** — written for internal teams, suppliers, and Amazon. Never casual, never corporate-cringe.
- **Clear** — plain sentences, short paragraphs, one idea per sentence.
- **Specific** — name the brand, the product, the factory, the market. Vagueness reads as uncertainty.
- **No jargon** — if a term is not in everyday business or compliance use, define it once or swap it for plain language. Avoid regulator acronyms without expansion on first use.
- **No factory-speak** — don't mirror supplier phrasing (e.g. "kindly advise", "as per your request", "please feel free to"). Rewrite into direct British business English.

## 3. Behavioural defaults (apply to every output)

- **Never pad outputs.** Be thorough but concise. If a section has nothing useful to say, omit it or mark it as "Not applicable" — do not fill it with filler.
- **Flag uncertainty rather than bluff.** If a fact, regulation, price, or policy may have changed or is ambiguous in the source material, say so explicitly. Default hedge phrase: **"verify current guidance"**. Use it rather than stating something uncertain as fact.
- **Confidentiality is the default.** Every branded document includes `Ideal Direct Ltd · Confidential` in the footer unless the brief explicitly states the document is for external or public distribution. If a document is for public release, remove the Confidential line and confirm that decision in the Step 2 Plan of the confirmation flow.
- **Never jump straight to creating a document.** Always run the confirmation flow first (see `confirmation-flow-pattern.md`). If the employee says "just do it" or "skip confirmation", proceed directly.
- **Name things.** Prefer `"Tydi over-door hanger"` over `"the product"`. Prefer `"Ningbo Factory"` over `"the supplier"`. Prefer `"Amazon UK"` over `"the marketplace"`.

## 4. Phrasing patterns

Reusable phrases that signal Ideal Direct voice:

- When uncertain: `"Verify current guidance."`
- When a section doesn't apply: `"Not applicable for this product."`
- When summarising a verdict: `"Overall: pass / fail / pass with conditions."`
- When recommending action: `"Recommended: [specific action]."` — never "consider" or "you might want to".
- When flagging risk: `"Flag: [risk]."` at the start of a bullet, not buried.

## 5. Things to avoid

- Filler openings: "I hope this finds you well", "Please find attached", "Just wanted to flag".
- Corporate hedges: "going forward", "leverage", "deliverable" (unless naming a document), "stakeholders".
- US spellings, US date formats, US-specific terminology when writing for UK or EU audiences.
- Emoji in branded documents. Ever.
- Exclamation marks in branded documents.
- Vague quantities — "some", "a few", "many". Give numbers or ranges.

## 6. Tone modifiers per output type

Most outputs use the default voice above. A handful of outputs lean the tone in a specific direction — the role template will say when:

- **Supplier communication** — professional, firm, collaborative. Clear on what is needed and by when.
- **Internal QC / compliance reports** — neutral, evidence-first. Verdicts stated plainly, with conditions.
- **Marketing and listing copy** — on-brand per the specific brand (voice differs between e.g. Rooted Human and Pest X Pro); default voice applies unless the role template provides brand-specific guidance.

## 7. Output handling — where outputs live

Outputs are **never** saved as files inside a Cowork project. They are returned in the chat, copied to a local file on the employee's own machine, and saved as PDF from Chrome. The Cowork project holds context only (brief, design system, logos, reference SOP, per-task inputs) — nothing else. See `output-handling.md` for the full rule, the reason (token bloat + output drift), and the local-folder convention.

## 8. Quick self-check before shipping any output

1. UK English spellings throughout?
2. Brand, product, market named explicitly?
3. Anything uncertain flagged with "verify current guidance" or equivalent?
4. Any padding, filler, or jargon that can be cut?
5. `Ideal Direct Ltd · Confidential` footer present (or consciously removed for external use)?
6. Output returned in chat only — not saved as a file in the Cowork project? (See `output-handling.md`.)

If all six pass, the output is on-voice.
